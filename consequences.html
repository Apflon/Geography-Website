The consequences of overfishing

Food shortages

Due to overfishing almost a third of all fisheries are in danger of closing down. Since many people rely on fish for food, overfishing can cause food shortages. Overfishing of fish that are high up in the food chain, such as tuna, can cause an imbalance for the fish that are lower down in the food chain.

Marine Habitat damage

Overfishing causes massive damage to marine habitats due to the large nets that drag across the sea floor or overgrowth of algae due to predatory fish having to eat small herbivorous species, this destroys coral reefs which are essential to the earth as coral reefs generate half of all the earth's oxygen and also absorb a third of carbon dioxide released by burning fossil fuels.

Other marine life dying

Big fishing boats use massive nets to catch their fish, but these nets dont just catch the fish that they are trying to catch. They also catch other fish in the area and since these fish are not needed they are thrown out dead. This causes an unnecessary loss of marine life on a massive scale
