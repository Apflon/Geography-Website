Solutions to overfishing

Strict guidelines

The easiest way to reduce overfishing would be to enforce stricter guidelines and regulations on fishing to limit the amount and way that fish is caught, while this would cause prices to raise and some industries to be affected, in the long term this will greatly reduce the effects that overfishing has on aquatic eco-systems, which affects the whole world.

Marine protected areas

Marine protected areas, which are areas where fishing is banned or tightly regulated in, can be one of the most effective solutions to overfishing, increasing the size or amount of these marine protected areas will easily reduce the impact of overfishing and begin to restore ecosystems. However governments will have to be prepared for the loss of income that reduced fishing will cause.

What can you do?

You can help reduce overfishing by stopping or limiting your fish consumption, if there is less demand for fish then less fish will have to be caught reducing overfishing. While it might not seem to do much alone, if many people start limiting their fish intake it can make a difference.

You could also help by buying only sustainable fish. Look out for the MSC blue tick on certified sustainable seafood.
